# Informative modals

<aside>

Informative modals appear to communicate important information, updates, or guidance to users without requiring a confirmation or decision. Unlike confirmation modals, **they don't interrupt a workflow to verify intent** – they inform, educate, or alert users to something they need to know.

## How to write an informative modal:

#### Title

- State the situation as an affirmative — never a question
- Use sentence case
- **2–5 words: `Payment successful`** , **`Verify your email address`** , **`Monthly invoice limit reached`**

#### Body

- Follow this structure: **`What happened`** + **`what comes next` / `solution`**
- Keep in within 1–3 sentences
- Acknowledge the situation with empathy; skip the apology

→ **`We couldn't process your payment. Please check your card details`** vs. **`Something went wrong`**

→ **`Please check your details and try again`** vs. **`You entered incorrect details`**

→ **`Your payment of 1,250.00 EUR has been processed. You will receive a confirmation email shortly.`** 

#### CTA

- Single button, either closes the modal **`Close`**, **`Dismiss`** or takes the next logical step **`Verify email`**, **`Go to Settings`** , **`Try again`** , **`View invoices`**
- Never use **`OK`**
