# CTA buttons

---

## How to write a CTA:

- **Use infinitive verbs** to describe **what happens when clicked**: `Send invoice` `Delete account`
- Multi-step flows use `Continue`
- Final step of the flow use the action verb of the outcome, such as `Create` `Confirm` `Send`
- If you have enough space, contextualise the action: `Save changes` instead of `Save` , `Add contact` instead of `Add`.
- Use the same verb for the same action across the product.

## Most common CTA

Multi-steps flow:

| 🇬🇧 | 🇫🇷 | 🇩🇪 | 🇩🇰 | 🇳🇱 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`Continue`** | **`Continuer`** | **`Weiter`** | **`Fortsæt`** | **`Doorgaan`** |

Creation (of anything the user shapes):

| 🇬🇧 | 🇫🇷 | 🇩🇪 | 🇩🇰 | 🇳🇱 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`Create`** | **`Créer`** | **`Erstellen`** | **`Opret`** | **`Aanmaken`** |

Addition (of something out of the users’ control: physical person, company, team member):

| 🇬🇧 | 🇫🇷 | 🇩🇪 | 🇩🇰 | 🇳🇱 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`Add`** | **`Ajouter`** | **`Hinzufügen`** | **`Tilføj`** | **`Toevoegen`** |

Secondary CTA to cancel the ongoing action: 

| 🇬🇧 | 🇫🇷 | 🇩🇪 | 🇩🇰 | 🇳🇱 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **`Return`** | **`Retour`** | - | - | - |
