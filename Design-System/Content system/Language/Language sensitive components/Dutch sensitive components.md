# Dutch sensitive components

## 🎼 Informal tone vs. Formal tone

- Use "je/jij" (informal) for all product communications
- Use "u" (formal) only in exceptional legal or compliance contexts
- Avoid overly casual expressions or slang but don’t be overly formal `Je factuur is succesvol verzonden.`  `Super! Je factuur is onderweg.`
- Keep language simple, clear, and conversational

## 🗓️ Numbers and dates

- Months and days of the week use lowercase unless sentence-initial `januari` `maandag` `jan`
- Thousands separator are space or full stop, decimal use a comma `1 250,50` `1,250.0`
- Ordinal format use lowercase “e” `1e, 2e, 3e, 4e.`
- Phone numbers use country code and spaces to group digits `+31 234 567 8901`

## ⚛️ Other sensitive components

- The VAT acronym is `btw` (all lowercase, except at the beginning of a sentence or if first word, then it’s `Btw`)
