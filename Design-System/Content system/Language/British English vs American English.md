# British English vs. American English

<aside>

**Our product uses British English exclusively.** 

This applies to all interface copy, documentation, help content, notifications, and communications. Consistency in language variety is essential for maintaining a professional, cohesive brand voice across all markets.

</aside>

## 💂‍♂️ Why British English?

British English is our standard because:

- We are a European product and our main markets are based in Europe
- It brings a more professional and trustworthy touch
- It provides consistency across our product family

## 🎓 Common spelling differences

### ise vs -ize endings

**British English uses -ise:**

- ✅ Authorise, recognise, organise, customise, analyse
- ❌ Authorize, recognize, organize, customize, analyze (American)
    
    <aside>
    
    **Note:** Some words always use -ize in both variants (e.g. capsize, seize), but when in doubt, use -ise.
    
    </aside>
    

→ `Authorise this transaction`

### our vs -or endings

**British English retains the 'u':**

- ✅ Colour, favour, behaviour, honour, labour
- ❌ Color, favor, behavior, honor, labor (American)

→ `Customise your invoices with your company’s colours`

### re vs -er endings

**British English uses -re:**

- ✅ Centre, metre, litre, fibre
- ❌ Center, meter, liter, fiber (American)

→ `Help centre`

### ogue vs -og endings

**British English retains -ogue:**

- ✅ Catalogue, dialogue, analogue
- ❌ Catalog, dialog, analog (American)

→ `Items catalogue`

### **British English doubles the 'l':**

- ✅ Travelled, cancelled, modelled, labelled, signalled
- ❌ Traveled, canceled, modeled, labeled, signaled (American)

→ `Payment cancelled`

### Other common differences

| British English | American English | Context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cheque | Check | "Bank cheque", "cheque number" |
| Programme | Program | "Loyalty programme" (but "computer program") |
| Practise (verb) / Practice (noun) | Practice (both) | "Best practice guidelines" |
| Fulfilment | Fulfillment | "Order fulfilment" |
