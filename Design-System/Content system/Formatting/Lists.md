# Lists

## **Quick reference**

| **Item length** | **Capital** | **Full stop** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Full sentence | Yes | Yes |
| Short phrase (4+ words) | Yes | No |
| 1–3 words | No | No |
- Always introduce a list with a sentence ending in a colon
- Use numbered lists only when order matters

## How to write a list:

#### IF list is within a paragraph:

- Introduce with a complete sentence ending in a colon
- Keep items parallel: same structure, same length, same tone
- No repeated words if they add nothing: **`the`**, **`a`**, **`your`**

<aside>

**Please prepare:**

- **credentials**
- **proof of residence**
</aside>

<aside>

- You will need your identity papers.
- You will also need a proof of residence.
</aside>

#### IF list is introducing features:

- No full sentences, write items like titles (see [Titles](../Titles%20314f148b3ab7807fa215de44d10c6f98.md))
- No capital letter, no full stop
- No article before the item: **`a`**, **`an`**, **`the`**
- If a feature has sub-details, add them inline after a comma
- No nested list

<aside>

**✓ 1 Mastercard Business World Debit**

**✓ Unlimited invoicing tool**

**✓ Advanced features: 20 virtual cards, 2 cheque deposits/month**

**✓ Exclusive insurance: legal aid, mobile screen break**

</aside>

<aside>

**✓ A Mastercard Business World Debit card.**

**✓ An unlimited access to the invoicing tool.**

**✓ Advanced features:**

**• 20 virtual cards included**

**• 2 cheque deposits/month**

</aside>

#### IF list is indicating actions to follow:

- Each item is an action
- Follow this structure: **`verb`** + **`clarify the document or destination`**
- If the list has a title that already contains the verb, don't repeat it on each item
- Capital letter on each item, no full stop
- When the list has a title containing the verb, drop the verb from each item

<aside>

**1** Keep your identity papers close to you

**2** Download your proof of residence

**3** Input the email for each recipient

</aside>

<aside>

**Complete your profile**

**`Identity papers`** 

**`Address`**

</aside>

<aside>

**1** First, you'll need your identity papers.

**2** And your proof of residence

**3** An email has to be sent to each recipient.

</aside>

<aside>

**Complete your profile**

**`Upload your identity papers`**

**`Add your address`**

</aside>

## Go further

### **Why drop the verb when there's a title?**

Repeating the verb on every item ("Upload your identity papers", "Add your address") creates redundancy and visual noise. The title already carries the action so the items just need to name the object. This also makes lists scannable faster.

### **Maximum list length**

Avoid listing more than 7 items. Beyond that, consider grouping into categories or restructuring as a separate flow or page.

### **No nested lists**

Multilevel lists reduce readability significantly. If a list item needs sub-details, add them inline (after a dash or comma) rather than indenting a second level.
